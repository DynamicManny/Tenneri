The situation

Most online courses were not designed.
They were uploaded.

If any of these resonate, we have the tools you need.

01

"Social media and AI have changed how students absorb information. The course has not changed with it."

The same student who cannot focus through a 40-minute lecture will spend three hours going down a YouTube rabbit hole. Attention is not the problem. The design is.

02

"Your course was probably built around the student who reads everything and takes notes. That student is a minority in most cohorts now."

Readers, watchers, and doers are all in the same room. When the path through the material only works for one type, the others quietly fall behind.

03

"You added a tool. Students treated it like optional extra credit."

When the experience lives outside the course, students leave it outside the course. The solution was never another tab.

What it looks like when it works

A poor launch.
A better second version.
Students who stayed.

A program launched with strong content but the wrong container. Students were dropping. Completion rates were low. The institution knew the material was good. The experience around it was not.

Version 2 was a full redesign. Same content, rebuilt as a structured multi-path environment. Readers had depth. Watchers had short-form summaries. Doers had scenario labs that put them inside the material before they were tested on it.

The redesigned program saw a 38% improvement in scores over the first version. Completion rates nearly doubled. Students said the material in version 2 was actually harder. What changed was that the path through it was clearer, so it felt more manageable rather than less demanding.

The stronger student outcomes meant the institution no longer needed the external platform they had been running alongside the course. We rebuilt everything in-house. They own it. No vendor, no renewal, no ongoing cost.

Built for the way the program actually runs, whether that is live, self-paced, or somewhere in between.

Ramano Stoute
Founder, Tenneri
The person behind the work

Teaching runs three generations deep.
Tenneri was built out of what I watched that inheritance fail to prepare us for.

I watched my mother during the pandemic try to recreate her classroom over Zoom. It was a miserable experience. Not because she was not good at her job. Because she was trying to replicate something in a medium that was never designed for it.

Online learning should not be a replacement for the classroom. It should be its own thing. Built for what it is: a space where the design of the experience carries the weight of the learning.

I figured that out through years of teaching, experimenting, and building things out of frustration because what was available was not good enough for my students. That frustration became Tenneri.

The lessons that stayed with me longest were never the ones with the most content. They were the ones where the structure itself did the teaching. One instructor stopped me mid-presentation and said: "It is PowerPoint, not PowerEssay." That line never left me. It changed how I present, how I write, how I design every course, and how I think about what deserves space in a learning experience and what does not. If it does not earn its place, it does not belong.

I am not trying to teach the world. I am trying to give educators the tools to make learning feel like something worth keeping.

Ramano Stoute
Founder, Tenneri  ·  Instructional Designer and eLearning Developer
