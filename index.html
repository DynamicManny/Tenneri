About Tenneri

Named to hold.
Built to last.

The name Tenneri comes from the Italian verb tenere, meaning to hold, to keep, to sustain. In education, this is a structural tenet.

Learning should retain attention. Assessments should withstand real conditions. Platforms should maintain value long after vendor contracts end.

Tenneri began with a recurring institutional problem. External tools failed mid-semester. Licensing shifted without warning. Students completed work inside third-party platforms and disengaged from the LMS. The solution was to build directly inside the LMS so the experience and infrastructure remained aligned.

That work produced Fluid Adaptive Runtime (FAR), a framework for building interactive modules, applied simulations, adaptive labs, and fully functional system environments entirely within the LMS. Deployed in live courses. Designed for long-term use.

Results speak for themselves. A Governance, Risk, and Compliance course shifted from passive delivery to structured simulation. A data science program replaced an external lab platform with a native environment, reducing licensing costs by 68% while keeping students engaged in the LMS. These outcomes demonstrate the durability and effectiveness of the design.
